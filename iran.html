DAY 59 · US–IRAN WAR
Mon Apr 27 09:05 PT — Day 59. Hour-on-hour: State Dept on the record — Rubio says Tehran’s Hormuz-first paper is “not acceptable” to the US or others, with the bind being a reopen framing that has Iranian permitting attached to international waters: “What they mean by opening the straits is … you coordinate with Iran, get our permission, or we’ll blow you up and you pay us … They cannot normalize, nor can we tolerate them trying to normalize, a system in which the Iranians decide who gets to use them.” He flags Iranian leadership as “fractured,” with negotiators “often in disagreement with other factions,” and frames that as a structural ceiling on what the diplomatic track can deliver (Times of Israel). Araghchi at the Putin sit-down opens by telling reporters the US, the world’s greatest “superpower,” “has not achieved any of its war goals, and that is why it is requesting negotiations” (Al Jazeera) — the FM track posture-statement that pairs to Rubio’s rejection. Putin–Araghchi sit-down on the record in St. Petersburg — Putin: “Russia will do what it can to support the interests of Iran and other regional countries and help bring peace to West Asia as soon as possible,” says he received a message from Mojtaba Khamenei (TASS, ANI). Public IRGC pushback on the Hormuz-first paper from a senior commander — Tehran “has no intention of unblocking the Strait of Hormuz”; controlling it is “the definitive strategy of Islamic Iran” — the first uniformed-side rejection of the proposal Araghchi is pitching abroad.
